LEARN A LANGUAGE beta

Learn a language is a simple language app that helps you learn African Languages. Basically the app spits out random situational phrases most of which you will encounter on your day to day life, your job is to write a phrase in the text box which is as close as possible to the exact translation and if possible feel free to directly translate it(For greetings try typing in the required replies).

Here's some information to know before you begin learning Icibemba in learn a language.(Feel free to refer to this guide anytime you get confused, you can find it right at the bottom of the dictionary)

Prerequisites

Pronunciation

Don’t worry about being perfect. Just be close. You will learn more once you get there. Everything in this guide is written very phonetically, so follow this outline and you will be fine.

Vowels: ‘a’ – like the ‘a’ in ‘cat’
‘e’ – like the ‘a’ in ‘mate’
‘i’ – like the ‘i’ in ‘ski’
‘o’ – like the ‘oa’ sound in ‘coat’
‘u’ – like the ‘oo’ sound in ‘cool’

Most consonants are pronounced the same as in English. There are a few exceptions:

Consonants: ‘c’ – like the ‘ch’ in ‘chair’
‘b’ – like a mix of ‘b’, ‘w’, and ‘v’ (try saying all three at once)
‘ ng’ ‘ – like the ‘ng’ in ‘singing’

Greetings

"Hellos"

Icibemba English When to use
Greeting: Mwashibukeni? Good morning? Morning
Response: Eya mukwai. Yes mukwai. Mexico
Greeting: Mwaikaleni? You’ve been good? Afternoon
Response: Eya mukwai. Yes mukwai. UK
Greeting: Cungulo mukwai. Good evening mukwai? Evening
Response: Eya mukwai. Yes mukwai. Italy
Greeting: Mwapoleeni? Hello? Anytime
Response: Eya mukwai. Yes mukwai.
Greeting: Mwabombeni? Work’s good? When someone’s working.
Response: Eya mukwai. Yes mukwai.

"How are yous"

Icibemba English When to use
Greeting: Muli Shaani? How are you? Anytime
Response: Bwino. Well. Mexico
Greeting: Mwashibukashaani? How did you sleep? Morning
Response: Bwino. Well. UK
Greeting: Mwaikalashaani Afternoon
Response: Bwino. Well.
Greeting: Mwabombashaani? How has work been? At/after work.
Reponse: Bwino. Well
Greeting: Kung’anda Kulishaani? How’s home? Anytime.
Response: Kung’anda Kuliye Bwino Home is well.
